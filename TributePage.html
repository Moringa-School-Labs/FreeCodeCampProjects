Mzee Jomo Kenytatta

The Man of the People



Jomo Kenyatta was a Kenyan anti-colonial activist and politician who governed Kenya as the first President.


Here is a time line of Mzee Jomo Kenyatta:

  • 1891 - Kenyatta was born in the village of Nginda.
  • 1909 - Enrolled as a pupil at the Church of Scotland Mission (CSM) at Thogoto.
  • 1914 - Kenyatta moved to Thika, where he worked for an engineering firm run by Briton John Cook.
  • 1922 - He joined Kikuyu Central Association.
  • 1929 - Kenyatta sailed from Mombasa to Britain, London.
  • 1933 - Worked as a linguistic informant for the Phonetics Department at University College London.
  • 1946 - He became the President of the Kenya African Union
  • 1952 - Arrested together with five senior KAU members, charged with masterminding the Mau Mau, a proscribed group.
  • 1961 - Released form imprisonment.
  • 1963 - Kenyatta was sworn in as prime minister of the autonomous Kenyan government.
  • 1964 - He became the president of the Republic of Kenya.
  • 1978 - he died of a heart attack in the State House, Mombasa.
